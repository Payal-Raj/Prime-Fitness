Sports & Fitness

Benefits of Sports

When you hear the word sports you probably think basketball, baseball, or football. When you read fitness you may imagine intense daily workouts at a gym. As a person with a bleeding disorder you may not be able to participate in these activities. The next time you hear the words sports and fitness, consider that physical activity—through safe sports and exercises—will strengthen your musculoskeletal system and reduce extra weight. The real benefit: your joints will become more stable and you’ll be less likely to have bleeds and pain.

Playing Sports Makes You Happier

Life is difficult, and positive and negative emotions are constantly around us. Many people cope with difficult situations by binge-watching television, overeating, or working on themselves. One way to boost your mood, feels better about yourself, and reduces stress, anxiety, and depression is to exercise. Sporting activity promotes changes in the area of the brain that controls anxiety and stress. Sporting activities increase serotonin and nor epinephrine sensitivity in the brain, which relieves depression.

Sports are a Fun Way to Lose Weight.

The obesity epidemic affects millions of people worldwide, and our current way of life is to blame. We spend 9 to 10 hours a day in a cubicle at work, then return home worn out to eat whatever is on the table. This routine increases your chances of gaining weight and thus leads to hypertension and heart disease. Participating in sports is one of the best and most enjoyable ways to combat obesity. Physical activity helps you control your weight.Remember that excess body fat contributes to obesity and that working out can lower body fat. The only issue with exercising is that it wears you out and overwhelms you because of the difficult and heavy weight lifting that goes into it.

Helps to maintain your mental health

Stress is a part of every working individual out there in the world. Everyone, whether they are employed or not, experiences stress daily. Playing a sports activity can help you reduce stress in your life. Playing sports increases your heart rate and improves blood flow to your brain. This, in turn, stimulates the production of hormones that activate the growth of brain cells.

Sports Helps in improving Sleep Quality.

Regular exercise can help you get better quality sleep. Physical activity depletes energy, stimulating a recuperative process during sleep. Exercise raises body temperature, improving sleep quality by lowering body temperature while sleeping. According to studies, exercising for at least 150 minutes a week can improve the quality of your sleep by up to 65%.

Helps in Muscle Training

Playing sports is one of the best ways to have a proper muscle workout. It is enjoyable to play sports as it does not feel like a chore, and at the same time, they give you strong and toned muscles. You can tone your muscles by regularly playing basketball, soccer, football, tennis and baseball. By playing sports, you gain lean muscle mass and burn fat simultaneously. For gaining toned muscles, you must prefer sports that involve the movement of most of your muscle groups.
